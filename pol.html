THE KILLING OF Atty. Joshua Lavega Abrina

Atty. Joshua Lavega was the officer in charge of the Administrative Division of the Port Management Office of Palawan. He was known as a man of integrity and an unwavering commitment in protecting the learners right and strengthening the department's legal foundations.


He was shot by an unidentified gunman in his home in Brgy. San Jose, Puerto Princesa Palawan, as he was unloading belongings from his vehicle. He was declared dead on arrival at the hospital and rewards have been offered for any information that can lead to the resolution of the case. The Police Office of Puerto Princesa has formed an investigative team to focus on the case. Motive has not been established and confirmed, but speculations arising that it's the combination of his role in DepEd and PPA that triggered his assassination.


Killings of legal professionals can raise concerns in the safety and rule of law of the officers of the court. For the local community, this creates fear and uncertainty for the safety both for the public servants and citizens.

The Senate Blue Ribbon Committee

The Senate Blue Ribbon Committee or the Committee on Accountability of Public Officers and investigations is considered one of the most powerful committees in the senator wide jurisdiction over investigations on graft, corruption, and matters of public interest.

The committee has been investigating the alleged systematic and large-scale corruption in the flood control infrastructure projects. This includes the "ghost and substandard projects" and massive kickbacks. What makes this controversial is because of the alleged participation of the people from the executive and legislative branches of the government. Hence, the integrity partiality and credibility of the committee is at stake. The billions of pesos budget allocated for the flood control project and the impact on public safety makes this more than just a financial scandal.


The committee's ability to conduct impartial investigations should not be compromised and be perceived as partisan or biased. Reforms may follow on how infrastructure projects are awarded and monitored.
